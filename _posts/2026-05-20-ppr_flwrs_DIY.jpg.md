A bouquet is born from deaths.  
By severing a handful of flowers  
From their stems. Taking their lives as debt,  
And arranging their colors as per the dialect.   

Indulging in their ambrosial scent  
Is inhaling a ghost itself, just unapparently.   
Which is explanatory of the belief of why  
Love is just another way chosen to die.  

Because decay these flowers will  
Eventually, just how we would at the end.  
Be not upset, I'll create paper flowers for you  
Of black, gray and more of my muted hues.  

They will not wither, shall you keep them from rain.  
They'll be born of discarded paper, colored by hand.   
They'll smell just like your endearing self  
Shall you hold them long enough to your chest.   

We truly own nothing in this life, we're mere containers.  
But my affection for you will always be mine.  
And the flowers will be wrapped in this feeling    
That you'll hold in your hands, your soul gleeing.  

So I'll learn how to craft them for you  
If you so verily happen to like flowers.  
I'll glue and fold them with my fingertips   
And wait for them to caress your chin and lips.
